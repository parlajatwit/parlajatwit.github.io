Museum Madness

back

Museum Madness is a fast-paced minigame compilation. It was made for the IndieWare Jam, a game jam based around the WarioWare games from Nintendo, scoring 2nd place.
You can download it here, or just watch the video below.
My role was the lead programmer and team leader. I worked with a group of a few artists and musicians, brainstorming minigame ideas with them and outlining art and sound assets to be created for implementation.
There are around 10 minigames in this game. They range from avoiding COVID at a supermarket to fighting the moon god by throwing axes, all done to try and steal a big gem from a museum.

Were I given the chance to make this game again, I would choose to use anything but Unity. Unity doesn't have great support for HTML5 exports, so it's not easy to make well-performing web games with it.
When doing a game jam, most users tend not to download any executables. They would rather play a game in their browser, which seems to be the future for small, free games.
I would love to revisit this concept, rewriting it in GameMaker Studio 2 or a JavaScript game engine I've been chipping away at.
